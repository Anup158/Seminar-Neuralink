## üìñ Overview

This repository serves as a central hub for educational and research materials focused on Neuralink technology and the broader concept of the Internet-of-Brains (IoB). It contains a seminar presentation, a detailed report, and two critical research papers exploring the theoretical foundations, potential applications, and emerging security challenges, including malware and novel attack vectors within IoB ecosystems. Designed for students, researchers, and enthusiasts, this collection offers deep insights into brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) and their implications.

## ‚ú® Contents & Topics Covered

This repository contains the following key documents:

-   **`Seminar Anup[1][1].pptx`**: The main presentation slides for the seminar, providing a structured overview of Neuralink and the Internet-of-Brains.
    *   *Topics*: Introduction to Neuralink, BCI fundamentals, vision for IoB, current challenges, and future prospects.

-   **`Report.pdf`**: A comprehensive report summarizing research findings, analysis, and conclusions related to the seminar's themes.
    *   *Topics*: Detailed exploration of Neuralink's technology, ethical considerations, societal impact, and future directions.

-   **`The_Internet_of_Brain_Things_Theoretical_Basis_for_the_Usage_of_Neuralink_Chip.pdf`**: A research paper delving into the theoretical underpinnings and foundational principles behind integrating Neuralink-like chips into the Internet-of-Brains framework.
    *   *Topics*: Brain-computer interface theory, neural data processing, conceptual models for IoB, and potential applications of neural implants.

-   **`Position_On_Potential_Malware_amp_New_Attack_Vectors_for_Internet-of-Brains_IoB.pdf`**: A critical paper discussing the emerging security landscape for the Internet-of-Brains, identifying potential malware threats and novel attack vectors specific to neural interface devices.
    *   *Topics*: Cybersecurity for BCIs, neural data privacy, vulnerability analysis of IoB devices, and strategies for mitigating brain-specific malware.
